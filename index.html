About Bodh Gaya

Budh Gaya, in the Indian state of Bihar, is one of the world’s most important Buddhist pilgrimage centres. It is the place where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment beneath the sacred Bodhi Tree. Today, pilgrims, monks, and travellers from all over the world gather here to meditate, learn, and experience the spiritual atmosphere of this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Location: Bihar, India
  • Spiritual Significance: Site of Buddha’s enlightenment
  • Language: Hindi, English, plus many international visitors
  • Climate: Pleasant and cool October-March; very hot April-June; monsoon July-September
  • Must-try Dishes: Litti chokha, samosas, momos, and sweets from local stalls
  • Fun Fact: The Mahabodhi Temple Complex is over 1,500 years old and still a living centre of worship and meditation

Plan Your Budh Gaya Trip

  • Best Time to Visit: October to March for cool, pleasant weather and lively festivals.
  • Getting There: The nearest airport is Gaya (12km); Gaya Junction is the main railhead. Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and cycle rickshaws are available locally.
  • Where to Stay: Choose from guesthouses, monastery stays, budget hotels, and a few mid-range options.
  • Food & Drink: Enjoy Bihari food, Indian vegetarian fare, and Tibetan/Thai dishes at cafes and markets.
  • Etiquette: Dress modestly, remove shoes in temples, ask before taking photos of monks or prayer spaces.
  • Safety: Budh Gaya is generally safe, but take care with belongings and avoid isolated areas at night.
