Discover the Spiritual Heart of India

Welcome to Bodh Gaya

Bodh Gaya, located in Bihar, India, is one of the most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world. It is here, under the sacred Bodhi Tree, that Siddhartha Gautama attained enlightenment and became the Buddha.

Top Attractions

  • Mahabodhi Temple: UNESCO World Heritage Site, the main temple complex marking the spot of Buddha's enlightenment.
  • Bodhi Tree: The direct descendant of the original tree under which Buddha meditated.
  • Great Buddha Statue: An impressive 80-foot statue of Buddha in meditation pose.
  • Monasteries: Visit beautiful monasteries built by Buddhist communities from around the world.
  • Sujata Kuti: The place associated with Sujata, who offered milk rice to Buddha before his enlightenment.

Travel Tips

  • The best time to visit Bodh Gaya is from October to March.
  • Gaya Airport is the nearest airport (12km away).
  • Local cuisine and handicrafts are worth exploring in the town markets.
  • Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially at religious sites.

Getting There

Bodh Gaya is well connected by road, rail, and air. The nearest major railway station is Gaya Junction. Regular taxis and buses are available from the station and airport.

Plan Your Visit

Whether you are a pilgrim or a traveler seeking peace, Bodh Gaya offers a unique blend of spirituality, history, and culture. Plan your journey for a truly transformative experience!
