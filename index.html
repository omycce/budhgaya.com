Welcome to Bodh Gaya

Bodh Gaya is a serene town in Bihar, India, revered as the place where Prince Siddhartha attained enlightenment to become the Buddha. Rich in spiritual energy and home to ancient temples, Bodh Gaya is a must-visit for pilgrims and travelers seeking peace, history, and culture.

Mahabodhi Temple
A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this grand temple marks the very spot of Buddha's enlightenment and is the heart of Bodh Gaya.
Bodhi Tree
Sit beneath the sacred Bodhi Tree, believed to be a direct descendant of the original tree under which Buddha meditated.
Great Buddha Statue
Marvel at the 80-foot statue of Buddha in meditation, surrounded by beautiful gardens and carvings.

Sujata Kuti
Visit the peaceful site associated with Sujata, who offered milk-rice to Buddha just before his enlightenment.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather and vibrant festivals.
  • Getting there: Gaya Airport (12km) and Gaya Junction railway station offer excellent connectivity.
  • Local life: Try local Bihari cuisine and shop for handicrafts in the bustling town markets.
  • Etiquette: Dress modestly, remove shoes at temples, and respect local customs.

Plan Your Visit

Whether you are on a pilgrimage or simply seeking tranquility, Bodh Gaya offers a unique blend of spirituality, culture, and history. Wander among ancient monuments, meditate under the Bodhi Tree, and immerse yourself in the peaceful ambiance of this sacred destination.
