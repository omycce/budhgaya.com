Bodh Gaya Travel Guide

Sacred, serene, and full of history—Bodh Gaya is where Buddha attained enlightenment. Discover world heritage temples, vibrant monasteries, and a spiritual atmosphere unlike anywhere else.

What to Explore in Bodh Gaya

International Monasteries
Visit monasteries from Thailand, Japan, Bhutan, and more—each with its own unique style, ceremonies, and architecture.
Sujata Kuti
Cross the river to find Sujata Kuti, where Sujata offered Buddha a life-changing meal before enlightenment.
Meditation & Retreats
Try a meditation class or multi-day retreat at local monasteries or the Root Institute for spiritual insight and peace.
Markets & Street Life
Explore colorful markets for prayer beads, handicrafts, Tibetan bowls, and delicious local food like litti chokha and samosas.
Festivals & Ceremonies
Experience the energy of Buddha Jayanti in April/May or the Tipitaka Chanting Ceremony in December.
Pilgrim Life
Witness or join the rituals of pilgrims from across Asia, making Bodh Gaya a truly international town.

Plan Your Bodh Gaya Trip

  • Best Time to Visit: October to March for cool, pleasant weather and lively festivals.
  • Getting There: The nearest airport is Gaya (12km); Gaya Junction is the main railhead. Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and cycle rickshaws are available locally.
  • Where to Stay: Choose from guesthouses, monastery stays, budget hotels, and a few mid-range options.
  • Food & Drink: Enjoy Bihari food, Indian vegetarian fare, and Tibetan/Thai dishes at cafes and markets.
  • Etiquette: Dress modestly, remove shoes in temples, ask before taking photos of monks or prayer spaces.
  • Safety: Bodh Gaya is generally safe, but take care with belongings and avoid isolated areas at night.
